Orbitopedia: The destination for cosmos enthusiasts

Orbitopedia is the place to explore the vastness of the cosmos, from the origins of the universe and the wonders of celestial bodies to the latest discoveries in astronomy. Our meticulously researched articles cover a wide range of topics, from planets and moons to stars, galaxies, and beyond, accompanied by stunning visuals of the cosmos. We delve into the history of space exploration, the technology behind space missions, and the fascinating phenomena that shape our universe.

Orbitopedia continues to grow as an ever-evolving resource, largely supported by passionate astronomy enthusiasts. As a reader, you can help improve our site. If you spot an error, feel free to let us know. If you believe there's a missing topic, add it to our suggestion list. Your contributions and support help us keep expanding this cosmic knowledge hub!

Three Topics We Cover:

Planets and Moons: Dive deep into the fascinating worlds within our solar system and beyond. From the gas giants of Jupiter and Saturn to the rocky planets like Mars and Venus, we cover the diverse environments and characteristics of these celestial bodies. You'll also learn about the moons that orbit these planets, including our own Moon and the icy moons of distant worlds like Europa and Enceladus.


Constellations: The night sky is home to a rich tapestry of constellations, each with its own stories and significance. We explore both the well-known constellations, like Orion and Ursa Major, and lesser-known but equally intriguing patterns in the stars. Our articles provide insights into the mythology, history, and modern-day importance of these star groupings, guiding you on how to identify them in the sky.


Galactics: Beyond our solar system lies the vast and mysterious expanse of galaxies. From our own Milky Way to the distant Andromeda galaxy, we take you on a journey through these massive collections of stars, gas, and dust. Learn about their formation, structure, and the exciting discoveries astronomers continue to make as we peer deeper into the universe.
